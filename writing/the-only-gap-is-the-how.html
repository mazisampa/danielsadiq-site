The Only Gap Is the How

Most disagreements contain more alignment than we realize.

July 14, 2026 · 2 min read

Ask a leader where they want the team to be in twelve months. Most will describe something better: faster decisions, happier customers, stronger ownership, less waste.

That future often overlaps with the future you are trying to create. The disagreement is rarely about the destination.

The gap is the how.

When we argue too early about methods, we miss the alignment already sitting in the room. Start with the future. Name what both sides want. Then make the path visible.

Change gets easier when people can see that they are not being pulled away from their goals. They are being offered another route toward them.
